## Verified by direct read of the markdown (Brett/Claude, 2026-06-29)

- SFL is the engine of the positive program. Ch 7 has a section headed "7.2 Systemic Functional Linguistics." Term counts in the file: "systemic functional linguistics" 69, "Halliday" 79, "grammatical metaphor" 103, "metafunction" 34, "ideational" 49, "interpersonal" 49.
- Her own framing (intro, ~line 390): work in "systemic functional linguistics... can aid us in the positive project... [and] will play a central role in developing and defending the positive vision."
- Lineage (~line 364): Malinowski influenced both Wittgenstein and J. R. Firth, "who would become the teacher of Michael Halliday and the intellectual grandfather of work in systemic functional linguistics."
- (~line 131): "I had not yet discovered work on systemic functional linguistics at the time when I wrote *Norms and Necessity* (2020)."
- Definition of grammatical metaphor (~line 2162): "grammatical metaphors carry over a term from one grammatical category to another," quoting Halliday (2009, 126): "In grammatical metaphor, one grammatical class takes over from another." (This is the function-in-syntax framing she imports.)
- Functional motivation (~line 2187): "the primary motivation for nominalization is 'a functional one: by nominalizing we are able to do things with the text that we cannot do in unnominalized text'" (Eggins 2004, 95). (Framework-neutral; any grammar grants it.)
- The abstracta argument (~line 2419): placement/causal-access "barriers" fail because "we should have no expectation" of a tracking function "for nouns that enter language as grammatical metaphors." (The one inferential-looking use; but the work is done by the easy-ontology trivial inference, not the SFL framing.)
- Half-concession, fn 6 (~line 2315): grammatical metaphors "should not all be treated together, as their entry rules may work in different ways, deriving from different parts of language."
- Apparatus is "incomplete" (ch 9, ~line 2797): the metafunctional/grammatical-metaphor level "on its own does not enable us to distinguish the functions of different 'flavors'"; fix (~line 2837) is to identify the "base form" and examine "what needs and/or desires the base form serves" (Queloz-style; framework-neutral).
- The advertised payoff (~line 648): SFL "gives us some new reasons to think that the trivial inferences are perfectly acceptable." (Read critically: a functional motivation for the redundancy, not a new justification of the inferences.)
